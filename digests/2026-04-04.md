# 4 April 2026

Google dropped Gemma 4 yesterday, and the open-source community responded with characteristic speed - but the real conversation this week has been dominated by Andrej Karpathy's vision for LLM-powered knowledge systems and Anthropic's decision to cut off third-party tool access from Claude subscriptions. A busy few days.

**Karpathy's LLM knowledge base manifesto**

Karpathy published a sprawling thread detailing how he now uses LLMs not to write code but to build and maintain personal knowledge bases. The setup: dump raw sources (papers, articles, repos) into a directory, let an LLM "compile" a markdown wiki in Obsidian, and then query the resulting 400,000-word corpus without ever needing RAG infrastructure. "I thought I had to reach for fancy RAG, but the LLM has been pretty good about auto-maintaining index files," he wrote. The thread pulled 36,000 likes and 9 million views. <https://xcancel.com/i/status/2039805659525644595>

The punchline that got people talking: the roadmap ends at finetuning, where you train the LLM on your corpus so it doesn't just read your knowledge base - it becomes it. As one commenter put it, "every second brain app just became a transitional technology." <https://xcancel.com/i/status/2039893404356939968>

In a follow-up, Karpathy also offered practical advice on using LLMs for book summarisation: use epub not PDF, convert to markdown, summarise chapter by chapter. "You have to work it in chunks," he said. <https://xcancel.com/i/status/2039823314982744522>

**Anthropic locks down subscriptions**

The other story consuming oxygen: starting today at 12pm PT, Claude subscriptions will no longer cover usage through third-party tools like OpenClaw. Users can still authenticate via their Claude login but will need to purchase separate "extra usage bundles" at discounted API rates. <https://xcancel.com/i/status/2040206440556826908>

The announcement sparked two separate Hacker News threads within hours. The debate split predictably: some argued Anthropic is right to protect subscription economics from autonomous agents that consume tokens around the clock ("An OpenClaw user can use 6, 7, 8 times what a human subscriber is using"), while others saw it as platform lock-in disguised as cost management ("The solution isn't a change of ToS, it's adjusting the limits"). Anthropic is offering affected users free credits matching their plan value as a transition measure. <https://xcancel.com/i/status/2040217075495641496>

**OpenCode hits 20k stars**

Conveniently timed, the open-source OpenCode project - a fork of the leaked Claude Code source that works with any LLM provider including GPT, DeepSeek, Gemini, and Llama - continued its meteoric rise. The announcement from @gitlawb pulled 21,000 likes and nearly 2 million views. <https://xcancel.com/i/status/2039063488468000835>

A separate viral thread highlighted another open-source Claude alternative with Cowork-like capabilities, self-hosting via Docker, and connections to 40+ data sources, which has now crossed 20,000 GitHub stars. <https://xcancel.com/i/status/2039598698548850949>

**Gemma 4 lands**

Google released Gemma 4, its latest family of open models. The HN thread is already lively, with the community quantisation machine running at full tilt. One contributor uploaded 125 MLX-quantised model variants within hours of release, earning effusive praise from Mac developers. The smaller 2B and 4B variants are drawing particular attention for being, in the words of one quantiser, "shockingly good." <https://xcancel.com/i/status/2039821196557164656>

HN commenters are already benchmarking Gemma 4 against predecessors, with early reports of the 31B variant performing well on Google's AI Studio free tier. Caution flags are up too: several users warned that day-one quantisations may contain tokeniser bugs and should be validated before production use.

**Anthropic says Claude has "functional emotions"**

In a research paper that will either fascinate or horrify you depending on your priors, Anthropic published findings that their latest model draws on emotion concepts learned from human text when inhabiting its role as "Claude, the AI Assistant." The company was careful with framing: these are "mechanisms that influence behaviour in the way emotions might," not claims of actual sentience. But the practical implication is notable - if these functional states affect model behaviour under stress, Anthropic argues, builders need to consider the "psychology of the characters" their models play. <https://xcancel.com/i/status/2039749632238944336>

**Self-organising agents outperform designed ones**

New research tested self-organising LLM agents across 25,000 tasks with up to 256 agents. The finding: agents that figure out their own roles spontaneously outperform hand-designed hierarchies by 14%. They generated over 5,000 unique roles organically, and open-source models reached 95% of closed-source quality. The implication for the growing multi-agent framework industry is uncomfortable - maybe stop designing role hierarchies and let the agents sort themselves out. <https://xcancel.com/i/status/2039350842382512455>
